- **2026-05-16** — **Phase D dogfood round 2: triple-coherence + meta-policy + confidence-vs-triple findings.** Clean run against fresh scope `engram.m3.dogfood` on source thought `8a533e15` (a ~700-char mission statement with 6 propositions). Reflector emitted 5 facts at confidence ≥ 0.90 (none flagged); **statements were all faithful** (paraphrase-correct), but **4-of-5 triples were systematically broken** — inverted, partial, self-referential, or semantically drifted. Three new items filed under `## In scope > Pipeline quality` in `m3-search-quality.md`:

  - **[M] Triple coherence beyond lexical anchoring**. The v4 anchor rule ("subject AND object appear in statement") catches lexical-token mismatches but not semantic-relation mismatches. Concrete failure: `fact_id 66855ac2` — statement says "agent gives more weight to facts than thoughts"; triple says `(agent, are given less weight than, thoughts)`. Anchor check passes; relation is drifted. Four fact_ids cited as regression targets (`66855ac2`, `4e7a324f`, `4635bd9d`, `1104fb5b`, `4e4b08af`). Three fix paths: prompt-side coherence self-check (v5 prompt; cheapest); post-extraction NLI validator (most accurate; M5-eval-suite territory); two-stage extraction (slower; cleanest separation). Recommended path is prompt-side first.

  - **[S] Meta-policy / conditional clauses as first-class facts**. Source thought's "if unsure, the agent will ask the operator" clause produced no fact. v4's conditional-as-subject rule + episodic-skip negatives push the model away from emitting conditional clauses, but meta-policy / agent-directives are durable signals (the *opposite* of session-narrative). v5 prompt should distinguish "if a search was conducted" (transient) from "if unsure, ask the operator" (durable agent-directive) with positive + negative few-shots.

  - **[S] Confidence rubric should reflect triple coherence**. v4's rubric tracks statement faithfulness only; `66855ac2` scored 0.90 (clean-paraphrase band) despite the broken triple. Three paths: add triple-coherence to the rubric; split into two-axis confidence; or require null SPO when the model can't construct a coherent triple. Recommended is the third (composes with item 1's self-check).

  **Operator observation worth carrying**: "Consumers that rely on triple semantics (graph traversal, predicate filtering) will get wrong answers even when the statement is correct." The implication for retrieval ranking: trigram search over `statement || S || P || O` (Phase A) still works because the statement carries the real claim, but any downstream consumer doing structured-triple matching is brittle in proportion to v4's triple-quality gap.

  **Pattern across round 1 + round 2**: M3 was scoped as "retrieval quality." The dogfood is converging on **extraction quality** as the next-biggest problem. Polarity-contradiction (round 1), predicate verbosity (Phase C dogfood), and triple-coherence (round 2) are all post-statement, pre-storage extractor concerns. Worth a milestone conversation about whether these fit M4 (artifacts) or warrant their own milestone slot before M4. None are M3 regressions; all are accumulated v3/v4 prompt limitations + missing schema/validation infrastructure.
